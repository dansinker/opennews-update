We Want You To Hack the News

We're proud to announce that the 2012/13 Knight-Mozilla Fellowships are now accepting applications. If you're a developer or technologist interested in spending 10 months in some of the best newsrooms in the world creating innovative new approaches to the news, now is the time to apply!

What is OpenNews?

Knight-Mozilla OpenNews is about helping journalism thrive on the open web. It's about producing next-generation web solutions that solve real problems in news. It's about supporting communities of developers and journalists as they make, learn and invent together. And it's about deploying fellows—and code—into news organizations to collaborate and innovate in new ways. Join us! Here's how:

Fellowships

Our new class of 2012/13 Knight-Mozilla Fellows will be embedded in partner newsrooms around the world to spend the year writing code in collaboration with reporters and newsroom developers. Fellows work in the open by sharing their code and their discoveries on the web, to increase access to better software and ideas.

Hackdays

This year we'll be sponsoring more than a dozen hackdays to write code that helps to solve real-world journalistic problems. From Africa to the Americas, we're looking for partners to hack the news with us.

Community

The hacker-journalist community is a vibrant one, and all our initiatives are designed to help it grow in size as well as strength. Later this year, we'll be launching Source, a new site that highlights the best code (and coders) in the community.
